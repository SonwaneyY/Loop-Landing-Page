## 1. The Problem

As tech hiring leans on automation, employers experience **high volume but low relevance**, while qualified candidates are **excluded by the same automation**. Technology inadvertently increases recruiters' administrative workloads and promotes a lack of relevance in applications. Trust has eroded on the candidate side — hopelessness and a felt absence of empathy are the status quo.

**The focus: ghosting.** At the center of this dysfunction sits ghosting — the default silence companies return to rejected candidates, even after interviews.

**Why it persists:** The recruiting experience remains largely manual and overwhelming despite all the technology. Ghosting is a capacity failure, not a cultural choice (though Danny's mid-term critique — see Section 8 — argues otherwise and remains a live counter-argument).

### Key Statistics
- **61%** of candidates ghosted even after an interview — *Greenhouse, State of Job Hunting 2024*
- **53%** of recruiters report burnout from high-volume, low-quality AI-generated applications — *Joveo*
- **83%** of candidates believe their applications are never seen by humans
- **92–93%** of both employers and candidates distrust AI fairness in hiring
- **17%** of hiring managers have interviewed deepfake candidates
- **62%** of companies post ghost/fake jobs to make existing employees feel replaceable
- **30–50%** increase in application volumes in the last 24 months
- **$180,000** cost of a bad mid-level hire in tech
- **300–400** average applications per open role; recruiters manage 5–6 roles simultaneously
- **Recruiter P001 example:** 400 profiles reviewed → 15 received next steps → 50 "totally solid" candidates never contacted due to timing

> **Survey methodology note:** The 51-respondent survey should be framed as qualitative preference data, not statistically significant quantitative findings. It supports directional insight only.

## 2. Loop — The Product

**Loop** is an AI-powered CRM that manages the candidate rejection journey for recruiters. It integrates with ATS platforms as an intelligence layer — not a replacement — handling all post-rejection candidate communication autonomously.

**Scope:** Rejected candidate communication only. Not end-to-end hiring communication.

**Core principle:** *"Automate the administrative, not the evaluative."* Loop acts on decisions already made by humans — it never makes hiring decisions itself.

### How Loop Works: Four-Stage Activation Model

Loop activates after the first human round with the candidate.

| Stage | Name | What Loop Does |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Setup** | Training | Trained on org structure, company policies, team skill graphs, hiring trends, culture vs. talent profiles |
| **2. Analyze** | Evaluation Co-Pilot | Silent partner in interviews — analyzes candidate performance, helps hiring manager generate feedback |
| **3. Outreach** | AI Agent | Generates and sends personalized rejection email with feedback; updates ATS outreach log |
| **4. Converses** | Conversation Manager | If candidate replies, expands on context and manages thread to closure |

Loop reaches out autonomously, manages replies and follow-ups, closes conversational threads, and escalates to human recruiters only when intervention is required.

### Legal Design
All recordings are **anonymised and end-to-end encrypted**. Loop is trained on HR and comms policies. Feedback generation is treated as a legal and ethical act. Compliant with NY State **Automated Employment Decision Tools (AEDT)** law — humans make all decisions, Loop handles communication only.

## 4. Strategic Positioning

### The Strategic Shift (Post-Concept Testing)

| Dimension | Before Testing | After Testing |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Customer** | Hiring companies directly | ATS platforms (Greenhouse, Workday, Ashby) |
| **Scope** | End-to-end hiring communication | Rejected candidate communication only |
| **Product Positioning** | Standalone tool | AI Intelligence Layer on top of embedded ATS software |
| **Legal Risk** | Undefined | Anonymised end-to-end encrypted recordings; trained on HR comms policies |

### Business Model
Two paths — currently prioritising the ATS route:
1. **B2B to ATS platforms** — retrofitted feature layer; acquisition story ("$5/month upsell or acquisition story" — Talent Leader)
2. **Direct to employers** — standalone product (secondary path)

### Target Market
- **Entry point:** Series A+ companies (~50–100 employees). Smaller companies don't use ATS software.
- **Beachhead:** High-volume hiring contexts (100+ roles/year) — recruiter bandwidth thinnest, communication gaps widest
- **ATS market note:** Only 4 companies monopolize the ATS market — few targets, but each gives access to their entire client base

### Value Proposition

**For ATS companies:**
- Easy retrofitted solution requiring minimal development investment
- Competitive differentiator — no ATS currently handles the rejection journey
- Increased market share and feature edge over competitors

**For employers:**
- Reduced recruiter workload — Loop makes recruiters effectively omnipresent without manual sending
- Employer brand protection — ghosting damage contained before it compounds
- Retained talent relationships — declined candidates stay warm, not detractors

**Two operational goals (from Miro):**
1. Compress time-to-hire and reduce recruiter load in frontline, hourly, and high-volume environments
2. Make recruiters omnipresent without manual sending through automated communication sequences tied to pipeline stages

### Competitive Whitespace
No competitor handles the rejection journey. All market players target engagement and AI enablement for advancing candidates only. Templatized responses are the status quo for rejections. This is Loop's whitespace.

## 6. Research Evidence (Supporting Loop's Intervention Point)

The hiring decision workflow creates a structural gap: hiring managers consolidate scorecards — often from memory — after interviews. Rejection rationale exists but is never formalized. ATS documentation happens last, and feedback never reaches the candidate. This is where Loop's Evaluation Co-Pilot and Outreach stages intervene.

**Ghosting definition (Tim Young):** Ghosting only becomes a meaningful problem after face-to-face interaction. Non-responses to cold portal applications are not ghosting in the brand-damage sense. This validates Loop's activation point after the first human round — not at screening.

**Journey intervention point:** Loop sits where the recruiter's workflow ends and the candidate's silence begins. The aspiration is the Extend phase: rejected candidates who receive closure stay warm, may re-enter the pipeline, or recommend the employer.

## 8. Design Principles, Voice & Narrative

**North Star:** *"Automate the administrative, not the evaluative."*

**Copywriting principle:** Name the condition without revealing the solution. Generate curiosity before evaluation.
> *"Hiring technology scaled everything except the moment it mattered most — and Loop was designed for the silence it left behind."*

**Brand damage argument:** Ghosting is a brand liability, not an operational inefficiency. This framing resonates with talent leaders more than cost-per-hire or time-to-fill arguments. As AI consolidates the high-talent pool, ghosting damage compounds faster — candidates can more easily surface and share employer reputation signals.

**Writing voice:** Direct, systems-aware, lean. Passive-professional register with punchy endings. No fluff, no clichés, no idioms. Warm and humble, not formal or presumptuous.

**Visual benchmarks:** Stripe, Airbnb, Google, Apple

### Narrative Arc (Storytelling Canvas)
For presenting Loop to ATS companies and employers:

1. **Set the scene** — recruiter constraints, ghosting as the norm, communication breakdown at scale
2. **Big reveal** — Loop's activation model as the precise intervention
3. **Conclusion / CTA** — ATS integration framed as competitive advantage: *"those who humanize hiring today will own the standard tomorrow"*
4. **Why now** — personal experience as researchers and ghosted applicants; six months of primary research mapping both sides

**Target audiences for storytelling:**
- Ghosted applicants (the human cost)
- Constrained recruiters and hiring managers (the operational pressure)
- ATS companies and tech employers (the strategic opportunity)

**Delivery formats:** Pitch deck (primary), elevator pitch, explainer video

- **Community gathering:** Run Clubs, Third Spaces, Luma, Gary's Guide — belonging and communication rituals

### Recruiter Interview Themes
Three primary themes from recruiter research that directly shape Loop's positioning:

**1. Quality Over Quantity** — Recruiters present 3–5 curated candidates per week, not high-volume throughput. Validates Loop as a capacity-relief tool, not a volume amplifier.

**2. Intake Process** — Effective recruiting begins with deep role DNA understanding (org context, stakeholder expectations). The better a recruiter understands a role, the more meaningful Loop's rejection rationale will be.

**3. Ghosting / Communication Dilemma** — Automated rejections feel impersonal, but silence is worse. Recruiters know the silence is damaging but lack capacity to do better manually. This is Loop's precise entry point: the dilemma is not about will, it's about capacity and tooling.

### Hiring Interview Workflow (Design Roles)
Mapping of where Loop's Evaluation Co-Pilot fits within the interviewer workflow:

**Interview phase:** Candidates evaluated on problem solving, design critique, articulation/storytelling, collaboration ethics. Documentation methods vary: formal scorecards in ATS, informal notes, Slack messages.

**Decision phase (Loop's intervention):** Hiring manager consolidates scorecards — often recalled from memory, not structured records. Rejection rationale exists but is never formalized. Final summaries sent via Slack (informal) or ATS (formal). The step of recollecting feedback from notes/memory is the critical pain point Loop's Evaluation Co-Pilot addresses.

**Post-decision phase (Loop's primary output):** Rejection or advancement notification. In most cases, no substantive feedback is communicated — the silence Loop fills.

**Design challenge sub-process:** Recruiter sends challenge brief (problem statement, constraints, deliverables, deadline, evaluation criteria). Hiring manager/design panel sets rubric (problem framing, interaction design, communication, collaboration) and documents pass/fail rationale in ATS. Even in structured challenge workflows, feedback rarely reaches rejected candidates.

## 12. Financial Model — Key Outputs

Built March 2026. All figures are base-case projections.

**Revenue projections (base case):**
- 2026: $0 (pre-revenue capstone year)
- 2027: ~$18.5K (8 Starter + 5 Growth + 1 Scale customers; founder-led direct sales)
- 2028: ~$140.5K (~63 direct customers + 1 ATS license at $2,500/month)
- ARR at end of 2028: ~$244K

**Pricing tiers (value-based; direct-to-employer):**
- Starter: $99/month (1–3 recruiters; up to 300 comms/month)
- Growth: $299/month (3–8 recruiters; 800 comms; full analytics + Conversation Manager) — **primary beachhead tier**
- Scale: $799/month (8+ recruiters; unlimited; custom AI training; dedicated CSM)
- ATS Platform License: $2,500–3,500/month (flat fee per ATS partner; pilot target Q3 2028)

Pricing rationale: $99 sits below typical budget approval thresholds; all tiers deliver 8–22x ROI to the customer based on recruiter time saved and employer brand protection value. Growth tier is the strategic focus — 10x+ LTV:CAC, 4-month payback.

**Unit economics (Year 2 steady-state):**
- Gross margins: Starter 86%, Growth 91%, Scale 94%
- CAC: ~$1,060 (Starter/Growth), ~$1,690 (Scale)
- LTV:CAC: Starter 3.2x, Growth 10.3x, Scale 22x (industry benchmark: >3x)
- CAC payback: Starter ~12 months, Growth ~4 months, Scale ~2 months

**Cash & burn:**
- Monthly burn 2027: ~$16K; 2028: ~$59K
- Seed raise required for 18-month runway into 2028: **~$291K** (lean; pre-seed territory)
- Break-even ARR: ~$207K in Year 2 terms (approximately 37 Growth-tier customers or equivalent mix)

**OpEx structure (from Functions & Actions Tree):**
- 2027 total OpEx: ~$213K (personnel + COGS + G&A + S&M)
- 2028 total OpEx: ~$848K (first dedicated hires: AI Engineer, UX Designer added; CTO, Sales, CS hired by Q2–Q3 2028 post-seed)

**GTM path modeled:** Direct-to-employer (API/standalone) as primary 2027 path; ATS platform license as secondary path beginning 2028. The three GTM options evaluated were: (1) intelligence layer sold to ATS companies, (2) API sold directly to employers (modeled here), (3) standalone tool. Option 2 is the modeled base case; Option 1 remains strategically superior long-term but requires ATS partnership — a 2028+ milestone.
