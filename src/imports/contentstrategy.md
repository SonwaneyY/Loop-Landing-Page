## Strategic Frame

**Core reframe:** Ghosting is not a capacity failure — it's a brand equity risk. Companies pay to protect brand value. They don't pay to fix recruiter bandwidth.

**Problem → Cost logic:**
Silence after a face-to-face interview → candidate becomes a detractor → shares publicly → brand damage compounds → revenue impact (Virgin Media: $5.4M/yr)

**Loop's position:** The only tool that manages the post-rejection journey. No ATS competitor touches this space.

---

## Brand Voice Rules (Quick Reference)

- Name the condition before revealing the solution
- Use specific numbers — let statistics do emotional work
- End sections with a declarative statement
- Mirror recruiter language: "pipeline," "burnout," "brand damage," "ghosting"
- Never: "revolutionize," "game-changer," "seamless," "best-in-class"
- Never moralize — frame ghosting as capacity failure, not cultural failure
- Never overclaim — Loop handles communication, not hiring decisions

Loop acts only after a human has made a decision. It doesn't infer, speculate, or replace judgment — it handles what happens next.

**Stage cards:**

| # | Label | Title | Body |
|---|-------|-------|------|
| 01 | Setup | Training | Trained on your org structure, hiring policies, and culture profile — so every message sounds like your team, not a system. |
| 02 | Analyze | Evaluation Co-Pilot *(Phase 2)* | After the interview, Loop helps hiring managers turn scattered notes and scorecards into structured, compliant rejection rationale — before it gets lost to memory or Slack. |
| 03 | Outreach | AI Agent | Generates and sends a personalized, compliant rejection email — on-brand, logged to your ATS automatically. The candidate gets closure. The recruiter focuses on evaluation. |

## Copy Decisions Log

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| "Brand equity" not "capacity" framing | Danny's critique: companies won't pay to fix recruiter bandwidth. They will pay to protect brand value. |
| Third stat is ROI (8–22×) not another problem stat | Prove Loop works, not just that the problem exists. (Stripe lesson: outcome proof alongside problem proof.) |
| Virgin Media callout isolated, not inline | $5.4M in a paragraph reads as trivia. As a standalone callout it reads as evidence. |
| "The recruiter focuses on evaluation" not "the recruiter does nothing" | Positions Loop as augmentation, not replacement. Avoids threatening recruiter identity. |
| Section eyebrows carry the functional label; H2 carries the message | Stripe-style: eyebrow = orientation, headline = persuasion. Neither repeats the other. |
| No "revolutionize," "game-changer," or fluff openers | Brand voice discipline. Confidence without cliché. |
